Building Information Modeling (BIM) is a digital representation process used in the architecture, engineering, and construction (AEC) sectors to manage the planning, design, construction, and operation of buildings and infrastructure. By utilizing intelligent 3D modeling tools, BIM allows stakeholders to collaborate on a centralized platform that integrates graphical and non-graphical data into a single, coordinated model. This enhances decision-making across the project lifecycle, from conceptualization to demolition or repurposing.

The core features of BIM include 3D modeling, project simulation, cost estimation, scheduling (4D), sustainability analysis (6D), and facilities management (7D). As a result, BIM significantly improves accuracy, reduces risks, streamlines communication, and optimizes resources within the construction industry.
