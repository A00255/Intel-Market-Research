Ginger is a flowering plant widely used both as a spice in culinary applications and in the pharmaceutical industry for its medicinal properties. It is derived from the rhizome of the plant Zingiber officinale. In the context of the market, ginger is typically processed into different forms, such as fresh, dried, and preserved ginger, each catering to diverse consumer needs.

Ginger is renowned for its ability to treat a variety of ailments like nausea, indigestion, and inflammation. It is also used as a key ingredient in a wide array of food products, beverages, and medicines. With the rise in demand for natural remedies and functional foods, ginger has found a prominent place in the global market.
