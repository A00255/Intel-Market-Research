The downward trend in market valuation can be attributed to factors such as patent expirations, increased generic competition, and emerging alternative treatments.Metformin, widely marketed under the trade name Glucophage and others, is a first-line medication used for the treatment of type 2 diabetes, particularly in overweight individuals. It belongs to the biguanide class of drugs and is primarily used to lower blood sugar levels by improving insulin sensitivity and reducing glucose production in the liver.

Market Dynamics (Drivers, Restraints, Opportunities, and Challenges)
=
Drivers
-
•	Prevalence of Type 2 Diabetes: Rising obesity rates and sedentary lifestyles have significantly increased the prevalence of type 2 diabetes worldwide, fueling the demand for Metformin.

•	Widespread Use as First-Line Therapy: Metformin remains the most prescribed medication for diabetes due to its efficacy, safety, and cost-effectiveness.

•	Expanding Geriatric Population: The aging global population has contributed to an increased incidence of diabetes, further supporting market demand.

•	Combination Therapy Growth: Metformin is frequently used in combination with other antidiabetic medications, boosting its market presence.

Restraints
-
•	Patent Expirations and Generic Competition: The entry of multiple generic players has led to price reductions and lower profit margins.


•	Adverse Effects: Side effects such as gastrointestinal disturbances and the risk of lactic acidosis can limit patient adherence.


•	Emerging Alternative Treatments: Novel diabetes drugs, including SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 receptor agonists, are gaining popularity and impacting Metformin’s market share.

Opportunities
-
•	Rising Demand in Emerging Markets: Countries in Asia-Pacific and Latin America offer growth potential due to increasing healthcare access and diabetes prevalence.

•	Ongoing Research on New Formulations: Extended-release versions and combination therapies present new avenues for market expansion.

•	Government Initiatives for Diabetes Management: Health organizations and governments are actively promoting diabetes management strategies, indirectly benefiting Metformin sales.

Challenges
-
•	Regulatory Hurdles: Stringent regulatory policies for diabetes drugs can impact market expansion.

•	Supply Chain Disruptions: Geopolitical issues and raw material shortages may challenge production and distribution.
