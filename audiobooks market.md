Audiobooks refer to voice-recorded versions of written texts, typically books, that are available in digital audio formats. These are often consumed via smartphones, tablets, desktops, or specialized audio playback devices. Audiobooks offer users the convenience of listening to literary works while multitasking, driving, commuting, or relaxing. They cover a diverse range of genres including fiction, non-fiction, self-help, academic, and children's literature. This format has evolved significantly over the years with the rise of smartphones, streaming platforms, and digital media consumption, making it a popular alternative to traditional reading.

The growth trajectory is underpinned by increasing digital media consumption, rapid smartphone adoption, and evolving consumer preferences favoring convenience and multitasking.
