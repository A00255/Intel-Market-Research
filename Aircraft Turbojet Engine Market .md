An aircraft turbojet engine is a type of gas turbine engine primarily used to propel high-speed aircraft by expelling high-pressure exhaust gases through a nozzle to generate thrust. Unlike turbofan engines, which employ bypass air for enhanced fuel efficiency and noise reduction, turbojets rely exclusively on jet propulsion, delivering superior performance at supersonic speeds. These engines are compact, lightweight, and highly efficient at high altitudes and speeds, making them ideal for military jets, reconnaissance aircraft, and experimental aviation platforms.

The turbojet operates on the Brayton cycle, where ambient air is drawn into the intake, compressed via axial or centrifugal compressors, mixed with fuel, combusted, and expelled at high velocity. The resulting thrust propels the aircraft forward, with the core focus being raw power and acceleration. The high fuel consumption of turbojet engines compared to turbofans limits their use in commercial aviation but secures their relevance in defense and specific niche applications.
