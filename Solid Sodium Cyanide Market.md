The global solid sodium cyanide market was valued at approximately US$ 1,551.15 million in 2024 and is projected to reach US$ 2,206.02 million by 2031, growing at a CAGR of 4.96% from 2025 to 2031. This growth is driven by increasing demand in gold mining, industrial chemical manufacturing, and electroplating industries.

Solid sodium cyanide is an inorganic compound with the formula NaCN. It is a white, water-soluble solid that is highly toxic. Sodium cyanide is primarily used in the mining industry for gold and silver extraction. It is also utilized in various industrial applications, including electroplating, chemical manufacturing, and organic synthesis.

Market Dynamics
=
Drivers
-
•	Rising Demand in Gold Mining: Sodium cyanide is a key reagent in the extraction of gold and silver from ores. The global increase in gold mining activities is significantly driving the demand for solid sodium cyanide.

•	Growth in Chemical Manufacturing: Sodium cyanide is used as a precursor in various chemical processes, including the production of dyes, pigments, and pharmaceuticals.

•	Expansion of the Electroplating Industry: The use of sodium cyanide in metal finishing and plating applications is growing due to increasing industrial manufacturing.

Restraints
-
•	Stringent Environmental Regulations: Due to its high toxicity, the use and transportation of sodium cyanide are subject to strict regulatory controls, which can limit market growth.

•	Fluctuations in Gold Prices: The demand for sodium cyanide is closely tied to gold production. Variations in gold prices can directly impact its consumption.

Opportunities
-
•	Technological Advancements in Mining Techniques: The development of more efficient cyanide leaching techniques is expected to enhance its use in the mining sector.

•	Increasing Demand in Emerging Markets: Developing countries, particularly in Latin America and Africa, are expanding their mining and chemical production industries, offering new growth opportunities.

Challenges
-
•	High Toxicity and Safety Concerns: The handling, storage, and disposal of sodium cyanide require stringent safety measures, increasing operational costs for companies.

•	Supply Chain Disruptions: Transportation and availability issues, especially in remote mining regions, can create supply chain bottlenecks.
