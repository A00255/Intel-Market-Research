Military drones, also known as unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), are aircraft systems operated without a human pilot on board and primarily deployed for defense and military applications. These drones are remotely piloted or autonomously navigated using onboard computers. Their primary functions include intelligence gathering, surveillance, reconnaissance (ISR), and in some cases, direct combat operations such as missile strikes. With the evolution of drone technology, these UAVs are now equipped with advanced sensors, radar systems, high-resolution cameras, and even artificial intelligence capabilities to support real-time decision-making in combat environments.

Military drones vary in size and functionality, ranging from small tactical drones used for ground troop support to large-scale systems like the U.S. Air Force’s MQ-9 Reaper used for high-altitude, long-endurance missions. These technologies play a pivotal role in modern warfare, enhancing operational efficiency while reducing human risk in hostile territories.

The United States is still the biggest spender on military drones, with more than $13 billion set aside for drone programs in the 2024 defense budget. More than 100 nations worldwide have military drone capabilities, and China, Israel, Russia, and Turkey have become leading producers and exporters. Turkey's Bayraktar TB2 drones, especially, have become internationally renowned following their effective use in wars in Libya, Syria, and Ukraine. Furthermore, the implementation of autonomous drones, AI-powered integration, and swarm drone technology is gaining speed, indicating a transition towards next-generation warfare solutions. These trends highlight increased dependency on drones as a central feature of modern-day military operations.
