A ceramic dielectric waveguide filter is a type of filter that is used to select or reject specific frequencies in a microwave or RF signal.

These filters are typically made from ceramic materials that have been shaped into hollow rectangular or circular structures called waveguides. The waveguides are designed to allow certain frequencies to pass through while blocking others, depending on the size and shape of the waveguide and the materials used.
