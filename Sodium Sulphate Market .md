Sodium sulphate, also known as sodium sulfate, is an essential industrial chemical used primarily as a filler in detergents and in the manufacturing of paper, glass, textiles, and various chemicals. It is available in two primary forms: byproduct sodium sulphate and natural product sodium sulphate. This compound is highly valued for its ability to improve the consistency and performance of detergents, making it a staple ingredient in cleaning agents worldwide.

2. What are the key applications of sodium sulphate?

Sodium sulphate is widely used across multiple industries, including:

•	Detergent and Cleaning Agent Industry – As a filler in powdered detergents.

•	Glass Industry – Helps remove air bubbles during glass production.

•	Cellulose and Paper Industry – Used in Kraft pulping.

•	Textile and Leather Industry – Used in dyeing and finishing processes.

•	Others – Includes pharmaceuticals, chemical manufacturing, and water treatment.
