
The global amlodipine market was valued at US$ 13.1 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach US$ 17.5 billion by 2032, exhibiting a CAGR of 3.8% during the forecast period (2024-2032). Historical trends indicate steady growth in demand, driven by the rising prevalence of hypertension and heart diseases. The increasing geriatric population and expanding healthcare access in emerging economies also contribute to market expansion.

Amlodipine is a calcium channel blocker widely used to manage high blood pressure (hypertension) and angina pectoris. It is available in various formulations, including amlodipine benzene sulfonate tablets, amlodipine mesylate tablets, and levoamlodipine maleic acid tablets. This medication is essential in cardiovascular therapy due to its effectiveness in lowering blood pressure and providing kidney protection.
