Forklifts, also known as fork trucks or lift trucks, are powerful industrial vehicles used for the lifting, transporting, and placing of heavy loads over short distances. These versatile machines are primarily utilized in warehouses, distribution centers, manufacturing plants, and construction sites. Forklifts come equipped with two prongs (forks) used to lift and carry loads, typically on pallets, and can be powered by electric batteries or internal combustion engines. Various classes of forklifts exist based on their design, application, and power source, making them a fundamental component of modern material handling systems.
